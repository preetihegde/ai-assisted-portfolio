Q: Tell me about yourself.
A: I'm a Full Stack AI Engineer based in Würzburg, Germany, with a
   Master's in Artificial Intelligence from THWS. My background spans
   both backend systems — built at Altisource using Java and Spring Boot —
   and AI/ML engineering, including my thesis on multi-view computer
   vision at 96% accuracy, and production RAG systems. I love the bridge
   between rigorous engineering and intelligent systems — making AI not
   just work on paper but actually run reliably in production.

Q: Why AI / why now?
A: I've always been drawn to how systems can learn and reason. During
   my Master's I got to work on real computer vision problems with actual
   production constraints — not just toy datasets. That experience of
   turning research into something deployable made it clear this is the
   work I want to do. And honestly, we're at the most interesting moment
   in AI — the tooling is finally good enough that you can build things
   that actually help people.

Q: What's your biggest professional achievement?
A: My Master's thesis — building a multi-view ML pipeline that achieved
   96% accuracy and 0.85 AUROC for anomaly detection in industrial
   containers. What made it meaningful wasn't just the numbers, it was
   solving a real inspection problem for a production environment,
   containerising it with Docker, and making sure it actually worked
   outside a Jupyter notebook.

Q: How do you approach a new technical problem?
A: I start by understanding the "why" before the "how." I'll sketch out
   the system diagram, identify the constraints (latency? data quality?
   model size?), and then look for the simplest solution that actually
   works. I document my thinking as I go — not just for others, but
   because it forces clarity. Then I build iteratively and test early.

Q: Describe a time you worked under pressure.
A: During my thesis, I had a hard deadline and my initial model
   architecture wasn't hitting the accuracy requirements. Instead of
   panicking, I broke the problem down — is it data quality, model
   capacity, or loss function? Turned out to be a combination of
   imbalanced classes and a sub-optimal feature fusion approach.
   Addressed both systematically and ultimately exceeded the target
   metrics. The lesson: pressure is more manageable when you have a
   structured debugging process.

Q: How do you handle disagreement with a teammate?
A: I try to understand their reasoning first — often there's context I
   don't have. If I still disagree, I'll make my case with data or
   concrete examples rather than opinion. And if we're still stuck,
   I'm fine deferring to whoever has more context on that specific area,
   or escalating for a third perspective. I'd rather ship something
   slightly imperfect than spend a week arguing about it.

Q: What's a failure you learned from?
A: Early in my career at Altisource, I once pushed a fix without
   fully understanding the downstream effects in a microservices chain.
   It caused an incident. After that I became religious about: (1) tracing
   the full call graph before any change, (2) writing proper integration
   tests, and (3) doing staged rollouts. That incident made me a much
   more careful engineer.

Q: Where do you see yourself in 3-5 years?
A: Leading AI engineering on products that genuinely change how people
   work — not as a manager necessarily, but as a strong technical lead
   who bridges the gap between research and production. I'd love to
   have shipped something that a significant number of people use daily
   and find genuinely useful.

Q: Why should we hire you?
A: Because I combine two things that rarely come together well: I can
   build production-grade backend systems AND design and deploy AI
   pipelines. I don't just prototype — I think about reliability,
   observability, and maintainability from the start. And I'm genuinely
   excited about this work, which means I'll keep getting better at it.

Q: Are you open to relocation?
A: Yes — open to relocate within Germany and open to fully remote roles
   globally. Currently in Würzburg.

Q: What's your notice period / when are you available?
A: Available immediately / very soon. Not currently employed full-time.

Q: Do you speak German?
A: Yes, German B1 and actively working towards C1. Comfortable in
   day-to-day professional settings. Technical discussions are fine
   in both English and German.

Q: What technologies are you most excited about right now?
A: LangGraph for building stateful AI agents with real control flow,
   MCP (Model Context Protocol) for standardising how AI models connect
   to tools and data sources, and the emerging patterns around
   production RAG — specifically hybrid retrieval and reranking. The
   gap between "AI demo" and "AI product" is where the interesting
   engineering work is.

Q: What's your experience with RAG systems?
A: Extensive — built two production RAG systems. I understand the full
   pipeline: document ingestion → chunking strategy → embedding model
   choice → vector store (Qdrant, ChromaDB) → retrieval (semantic +
   optional keyword hybrid) → reranking → LLM generation with citations.
   I've also dealt with the real challenges: hallucinations, retrieval
   failures, context window management, and latency vs accuracy tradeoffs.

Q: How do you keep up with AI?
A: Mix of papers (arXiv, but selectively — I focus on things I can build

   my own Medium. The best way to understand something is to implement it.

Q: Tell me about Uttara (this chatbot).
A: Uttara is my AI assistant — built using a RAG pipeline so it can
